ExternalShapeAlign Class Reference

Detailed Description

ExternalShapeAlign implements the single particle alignment to the "shape" of the cell in tissue simulations. By shape, we mean the direction of the eigenvector of the cell's gyration tensor corresponding to the largest eigenvalue. For all particles we compute torque on the particle as , where is the coupling strength and is unit-length eigenvector of the shape tensor corresponding to the largest eigenvalue. Shape tensor is defined as the sum of the outter products of the edge vectors along the sided of the dual polygon (cell). It is addopted from M. Aubouy, Y. Jiang, J. A. Glazier, F. Graner, A texture tensor to quantify deformations, Granular Matter 5, 67 (2003).
