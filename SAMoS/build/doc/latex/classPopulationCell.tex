\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\doxyref{Population\+Cell}{p.}{classPopulationCell} class handles a basic model for cell growth, division and death. We set the division and death rates as well as rate of cell growth, defined as a rate at which cell\textquotesingle{}s native area grows. Cells are divided based on their current area and removed based on the their age, by a random event.

Particles are always divided along the direction of the orientation vector n.

\doxyref{Particle}{p.}{classParticle} division (emulates cell division) 

Divide cells according to their current area. We draw a uniform random number $ \zeta $ between 0 and 1 if $ \zeta < \exp\left(\gamma(A_i-A_0)\right) $, split particle. Here, $ A_i $ is current cell area, $ A_0 $ if the native cell area (which changes during growth) and $ \gamma $ is a constant. ~\newline


After the division, each daughter cell has native area equal to the half of the native area of the mother cell.

Position of daughter cells is determined by the orientation vector n.
