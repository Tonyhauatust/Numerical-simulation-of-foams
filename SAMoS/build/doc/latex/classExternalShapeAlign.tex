\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\doxyref{External\+Shape\+Align}{p.}{classExternalShapeAlign} implements the single particle alignment to the \char`\"{}shape\char`\"{} of the cell in tissue simulations. By shape, we mean the direction of the eigenvector of the cell\textquotesingle{}s gyration tensor corresponding to the largest eigenvalue. For all particles we compute torque on the particle as $ \vec \tau_i = -J \vec n_i\times\vec a_i $, where $ J $ is the coupling strength and $ \vec a_i $ is unit-\/length eigenvector of the shape tensor corresponding to the largest eigenvalue. Shape tensor is defined as the sum of the outter products of the edge vectors along the sided of the dual polygon (cell). It is addopted from M. Aubouy, Y. Jiang, J. A. Glazier, F. Graner, A texture tensor to quantify deformations, Granular Matter 5, 67 (2003).
