\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Enforces all particles to be on the surface of a Gaussian bump centred in the origin. Form of the bump is $ z(x,y)=A \exp\left(-\frac{x^2}{a^2}\right)\exp\left(-\frac{y^2}{b^2}\right) $, where parameter $ A $ controls hight of the bump and parameters $ a $ and $ b $ control the width of the bump in the x and y direction, respectively.

Computes normal to the surface. 

Compute normal to the surface at p

Normal vector is computed as the normalized gradient vector at point p. Coordinates of gradient vector are given as\+:
