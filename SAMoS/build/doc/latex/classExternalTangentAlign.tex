\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\doxyref{External\+Tangent\+Align}{p.}{classExternalTangentAlign} implements the alignment of the director to the tangent to polymer. For a bead in the polymer chain we compute torque as $ \vec \tau_i = -1/tau \sum_j\vec n_i\times\vec b_{ij} $, where $ tau $ is the coupling alignment time and $ \vec b_{ij} $ is the unit-\/length vector pointing along the bond connecting partiles $ i $ and $ j $. \begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
For this alignment to work, bonds have to be defined. User has to supply properly ordered bonds in order to ensure that computed torques have desirable behaviour, e.\+g., to align the director in the local direction of the polymer. ~\newline
 
\end{DoxyNote}
